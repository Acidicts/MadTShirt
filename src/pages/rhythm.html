Rhythm

When you combine any two or more notes or rests you create a RHYTHM

Basic Note and Rest Lengths

Semibreve (Whole Note)

4 Beats

Minim (Half Note)

2 Beats

Crotchet (Quarter Note)

1 Beat

Quaver (Eighth Note)

½ Beat

Adjacent quavers can be joined together with a BEAM

Semiquaver (Sixteenth Note)

¼ Beat

Adjacent semiquavers can be joined together with a BEAM

Dotted Notes and Rests

A dot placed after a note or rest tells you to increase the note or rest by HALF ITS ORIGINAL VALUE

Example: Dotted crotchet = 1 beat + ½ beat = 1.5 beats

Types of Rhythms

Crotchet Rhythm

This bass line would be described as having a CROTCHET rhythm

Dotted Minim & Semiquaver

Combination of long sustained notes with rapid passages

Polyrhythm

Two or more different rhythms with the same METRE played at the same time

Cross Rhythm

Two or more rhythms played at the same time but with conflicting ACCENTS often in different METRES

Ties and Triplets

Ties

A TIE joins two notes of different values

Here you would play the first note and hold it for 3 beats (2+1)

Triplets

A TRIPLET is 3 notes played where there is usually only space for 2
