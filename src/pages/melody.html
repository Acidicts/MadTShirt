    How High or Low the note is

    Interval

    The distance between any two notes

    Motif

    A fragment of a melody

    Range

    The span between the lowest and highest notes

    Phrase

    A longer melodic idea. Musical "Sentences" are constructed from phrases

    Melodic Movement

    Steps
    Movement between notes that are next to each other in the scale (adjacent scale degrees).
    Skips
    Movement equal to two steps — you “skip” over one scale note.
    Leaps
    Any movement larger than a skip (intervals bigger than a third).
    Scalar
    A section of melody that moves along using notes in scale order (consecutive scale tones).
    Chromatic
    Movement using semitone steps, including notes not in the key (chromatic tones).
    Passing note
    Non-chord tones that link chord tones, typically approached and left by step to connect harmony.

    Hook / Riff

    A memorable repeated melodic idea designed to catch the ear of the listener.

    Scale / Mode

    A group of notes which a melody is based on e.g. major, minor, blues, chromatic, dorian

    Countermelody

    A secondary melody that complements the main melody.
