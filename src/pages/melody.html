Melody

The tune of the music - a sequence of single notes that form a recognizable musical line

Pitch

How High or Low the note is

Interval

The distance between any two notes

Motif

A fragment of a melody

Range

The span between the lowest and highest notes

Phrase

A longer melodic idea. Musical "Sentences" are constructed from phrases

Hook / Riff

A memorable repeated melodic idea designed to catch the ear of the listener.

Scale / Mode

A group of notes which a melody is based on e.g. major, minor, blues, chromatic, dorian

Countermelody

A secondary melody that complements the main melody.

Compositional Devices

Techniques used to develop, repeat, and transform melodic material.

Repetition Repetition
Repeat a melodic idea exactly to reinforce familiarity and form.
Sequence Sequence
Repeat a melodic idea but start it on a different pitch (transposed repetition).
Imitation Imitation
Repeat the melodic idea in another voice or instrument, often offset in time.
Variation Variation
Alter the melodic idea slightly (rhythm, interval, ornamentation) while keeping its identity.
Ostinato Ostinato
Persistent repetition of a short melodic or rhythmic pattern beneath changing material.
Inversion Inversion
Turn the melodic idea upside down so upward intervals become downward (mirror motion).
Retrograde Retrograde
Play the melodic idea backwards (reverse the order of its notes).

Melodic Movement

Steps
Movement between notes that are next to each other in the scale (adjacent scale degrees).
Skips
Movement equal to two steps — you “skip” over one scale note.
Leaps
Any movement larger than a skip (intervals bigger than a third).
Scalar
A section of melody that moves along using notes in scale order (consecutive scale tones).
Chromatic
Movement using semitone steps, including notes not in the key (chromatic tones).
Passing note
Non-chord tones that link chord tones, typically approached and left by step to connect harmony.
