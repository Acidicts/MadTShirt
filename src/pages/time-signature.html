    • Time Signature

    A TIME SIGNATURE gives you information on how the beats are arranged in a piece of music. It is also known as METRE

    How to Read a Time Signature

    Number 2 on the bottom =

    MINIMS

    Number 4 on the bottom =

    CROTCHETS

    Number 8 on the bottom =

    QUAVERS

    Simple Metre

    Simple time signatures have beats that can be broken down into two notes

    2/4 Time

    2 4

    2 crotchet beats per bar

    3/4 Time

    3 4

    3 crotchet beats per bar

    4/4 Time

    4 4

    4 crotchet beats per bar

    Compound Metre

    Compound time signatures have beats that can be broken down into three notes

    6/8 Time

    6 8

    2 dotted crotchet beats per bar

    9/8 Time

    9 8

    3 dotted crotchet beats per bar

    12/8 Time

    12 8

    4 dotted crotchet beats per bar

    Compound Time Signatures

    In compound time signatures each beat is represented by a dotted crotchet which can be broken down into three quavers

    Common Time

    The vast majority of music is written with a 4/4 time signature

    Irregular Metre

    Sometimes music is written in a metre containing odd numbers of beats in each bar

    5 4

    5 beats per bar

    7 8

    7 beats per bar

    Simple and Compound Metre

    SIMPLE

    Simple time signatures have beats that can be broken down into two notes

    2/4 3/4 4/4

    COMPOUND

    Compound time signatures have beats that can be broken down into three notes

    6/8 9/8 12/8
